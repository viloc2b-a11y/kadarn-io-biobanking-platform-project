# PB-2.1 — Vision & Mission

> **Kadarn Product Book v2.0 — Part I: Vision**
> *The Institutional Capability Intelligence Platform*

---

## Why Kadarn Exists

In the world of biospecimen research, IVD validation, and translational medicine, a fundamental question goes unanswered every day:

> *"Is this institution ready to execute this program?"*

Answering that question today takes **weeks to months**. It involves collecting PDFs, verifying certifications, calling references, reviewing SOPs, and manually cross-referencing regulatory databases. For every new program — even a repeat program with the same institution — the process starts over.

**Sponsors** can't efficiently find qualified institutions. They rely on reputation, past relationships, and conference introductions. Evidence of capability is scattered across email threads, file folders, regulatory portals, and institutional memory.

**Institutions** spend enormous effort proving the same capabilities over and over again to each new sponsor. An IRB-approved biospecimen collection protocol that passed review for one program must be re-submitted, re-verified, and re-explained for the next. There is no cumulative record of demonstrated competence.

This is the problem Kadarn solves.

---

## The Industry Problem

The biospecimen and translational research ecosystem has unique structural challenges:

**Fragmented evidence.** An institution's capabilities are documented across IRB approvals, CAP/CLIA certifications, SOP libraries, equipment logs, training records, past program outcomes, and investigator CVs. None of these speak to each other.

**Reputation-based selection.** Sponsors select institutions based on who they know, not on systematically verified capability. This is inefficient, exclusionary, and risky.

**Manual re-verification.** Every new program triggers a fresh round of evidence collection. There is no portable, verifiable record of demonstrated readiness that follows the institution across programs.

**No standardized readiness language.** What does "ready for biospecimen collection" actually mean? Each sponsor defines it differently. There is no shared vocabulary, no common framework, and no way to compare readiness across institutions.

**Regulatory burden without strategic value.** Institutions maintain compliance documentation for audits but cannot leverage it to attract new programs. The evidence exists — it's just not organized for discovery.

---

## The Product Mission

> **Kadarn is the Institutional Capability Intelligence Platform that transforms how institutions demonstrate — and sponsors discover — operational readiness for biospecimen, IVD, and translational research programs.**

We replace reputation with **evidence-backed, explainable capability assessment**.

An institution that has demonstrated PBMC processing capability for three independent programs should never have to "prove it again from scratch" for the fourth. A sponsor seeking a biospecimen collection site with -80°C storage and GCP-trained staff should find qualified institutions instantly, with all evidence verifiable and traceable to its source.

Kadarn makes institutional capability:

- **Cumulative** — Evidence builds over time, strengthening readiness across all programs
- **Portable** — Capability demonstrated for one program applies to others of the same type
- **Explainable** — Every readiness assessment traces to specific, verifiable evidence items
- **Discovery-ready** — Institutions that prove readiness become discoverable by sponsors seeking those capabilities

## What Kadarn Is

Kadarn is a **platform for institutional capability intelligence**. It serves two primary audiences:

**For institutions** (labs, biobanks, clinical sites, CROs):
- Define your capabilities using a standardized taxonomy
- Upload and link evidence (certifications, SOPs, training records, program outcomes)
- Receive a derived, evidence-backed Readiness Assessment per program type
- See exactly what evidence is missing and how to close the gaps
- Make your readiness discoverable to sponsors when you choose

**For sponsors** (pharma, biotech, CROs, academic networks):
- Define program requirements in terms of required capabilities and evidence classes
- Discover institutions whose demonstrated readiness matches your program needs
- Review evidence — not just scores — before making decisions
- Monitor readiness changes across your portfolio of candidate institutions
- Make selection decisions based on verifiable proof, not reputation

Kadarn is also used by **regulators and auditors** who can trace capabilities to their underlying evidence, and by **platform administrators** who maintain the capability taxonomy and ensure quality.

## What Kadarn Is Not

Just as important as defining what Kadarn is:

**Kadarn is not a LIMS.** It does not manage laboratory workflows, sample tracking, or instrument data. It integrates with LIMS to surface evidence of operational capability.

**Kadarn is not a CTMS.** It does not manage clinical trials, patient enrollment, or study timelines. It answers the question that comes before trial execution: *is this site ready?*

**Kadarn is not a marketplace for biospecimens.** Kadarn does not list, sell, or broker biospecimens. It may, in the future, help sponsors discover institutions that have demonstrated readiness to provide biospecimens for specific program types — but the transaction remains between the parties.

**Kadarn is not a certification body.** Kadarn does not certify, accredit, or guarantee. It surfaces evidence and computes derived readiness assessments. The market — sponsors, regulators, program managers — decides what level of evidence is sufficient for their needs.

**Kadarn is not a consultant.** It does not tell institutions what programs to pursue or what capabilities to develop. It surfaces gaps and opportunities — the institution decides.

**Kadarn does not replace operational systems.** It integrates with them. An institution's LIMS, CTMS, ERP, QMS, and document management systems continue to operate. Kadarn connects to them as evidence sources, not as replacements.

## The Name

**Kadarn** — from the Hebrew *kadár* (כַּדָּר), meaning potter or one who shapes from raw material.

The platform shapes raw evidence — scattered documents, certifications, records, past outcomes — into a coherent, verifiable picture of institutional capability. Just as a potter transforms clay into vessels of purpose and utility, Kadarn transforms fragmented proof into readiness that institutions can build upon and sponsors can trust.
