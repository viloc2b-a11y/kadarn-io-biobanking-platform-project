## Introduction

Kadarn was never created to become another marketplace.

It was never created to become another CRO.

It was never created to replace biobanks.

It was never created to compete with clinical research organizations.

Kadarn exists because an important part of the life sciences ecosystem became invisible.

---

## The Problem Nobody Was Solving

For decades, the life sciences industry has invested enormous resources in accelerating scientific discovery.

Sponsors became larger.

CROs became global.

Biobanks expanded.

Technology improved.

Yet one fundamental problem remained.

Organizations with real access to patients became increasingly difficult to discover, evaluate and engage.

Thousands of qualified clinical sites perform exceptional work every year.

They recruit patients.

Collect biospecimens.

Execute protocols.

Maintain quality.

Pass inspections.

Support sponsors.

Advance science.

Yet when a study ends...

their experience often disappears with it.

Their reputation becomes trapped inside individual projects.

Inside CRO networks.

Inside broker relationships.

Inside proprietary databases.

The site starts again.

Again.

And again.

---

## What We Learned

Kadarn was born after hearing something that should never happen.

An international life sciences company explained that finding qualified clinical sites in the United States had become extraordinarily difficult.

Not because those sites did not exist.

Because they could not see them.

The market had become so opaque that they were forced to work through intermediaries simply to discover organizations capable of performing the work.

That conversation exposed something much larger.

The industry was not suffering from a shortage of capable organizations.

It was suffering from a shortage of visibility.

## The Defining Sentence

Kadarn does not verify organizations.

Kadarn verifies institutional capability accumulated over time.

*Kadarn no verifica organizaciones. Kadarn verifica la capacidad institucional acumulada a lo largo del tiempo.*

Because an organization can change.

It can grow.

It can bring in new researchers.

It can open a lab.

It can lose certifications.

It can improve.

What Kadarn verifies is not a photograph.

It is a trajectory.

We are not building a system to say "this site is good."

We are building a system capable of demonstrating, with evidence and continuity, how that site became good and how it continues to evolve.

That difference is, in our conviction, one of the greatest strategic differentiators of Kadarn.
