# Kadarn Platform Constitution v1.0

**Status:** Operating constitution for PCP execution  
**Scope:** Conceptual rules already demonstrated by repository evidence  
**Non-scope:** Architecture redesign, API design, schema design, feature expansion

Kadarn is approved to move from Platform Discovery into Operational Consolidation. This approval does not mean the platform is finished. It means the main risk is no longer architectural uncertainty; the main risk is failing to finish the current platform into a product a customer can use every day.

## Constitutional Rules

1. **Organization is the persistent anchor.** Institution is the product reading of an organization when Kadarn assesses, discovers, profiles, or presents it.
2. **Claims are the fundamental unit of Evidence Core.** They are evidence-backed statements that can be supported, challenged, evaluated, versioned, and projected.
3. **Discovery produces candidate knowledge.** Discovery sessions, runs, artifacts, outputs, and curation do not become core truth until promoted into the evidence/claim lifecycle.
4. **Evidence is not silently edited.** Evidence is immutable, superseded, disputed, resolved, or replaced through lifecycle and provenance.
5. **Confidence is derived.** Confidence may be cached or snapshotted, but the primary truth remains the underlying claims, evidence, relationships, and provenance.
6. **Projections are not sources of truth.** Published Views, Passports, Evidence Packs, Discovery Reports, Institution Profiles, KPEs, and dashboards package source records for an audience.
7. **Portfolio defines access scope.** Sponsor portfolios and portfolio memberships determine which institutions a sponsor may inspect; they do not define institutional truth.
8. **Policy governs visibility and operations.** Effective permission is layered across auth, membership, RLS, policy evaluation, visibility rules, and projection filtering.
9. **Provenance must travel with evidence and projections.** A claim, evidence item, published view, passport, or report is incomplete if its origin and transformation path cannot be reconstructed.
10. **Consolidation comes before expansion.** Kadarn must finish, harden, normalize, and prove the current platform before adding speculative capabilities.

## Operating Decision

PCP execution must protect these rules. Engineering work should finish incomplete layers, remove production mocks and stubs, normalize vocabulary, prove end-to-end workflows, and harden deployment paths. Work that conflicts with this constitution should be deferred until the production platform is stable.

## Executive Verdict

Kadarn has a clearly defined technical and conceptual identity. The platform should now be managed as an operational consolidation program: not to imagine Kadarn again, but to finish converting an already solid architecture into a reliable production product.
